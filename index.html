Does Heat Destroy the Vitamin C in Your Sports Drink?
Quantified how heating Gatorade Fit across five temperatures (22°C–100°C) degrades its vitamin C content using iodometric titration — finding a 69.5% reduction at boiling point following an exponential decay pattern, raising questions about nutritional label accuracy under real-world storage conditions.

2025
Does Moving Weight Outward Make a Fidget Spinner Spin Longer?
Designed a pendulum rig to apply a consistent force to a fidget spinner across four weight-radius configurations (0–3 cm), then measured spin time for each. The result contradicted the initial hypothesis — spin time dropped from 15.2 s to 7.2 s as radius grew. Used rotational kinetic energy equations to show why: the larger moment of inertia drastically reduced angular velocity (107.5 to 11.7 rad/s), leaving too little energy to overcome friction and air resistance.

Did the Ancient Greeks Use the Golden Ratio to Build the Parthenon?
Used coordinate geometry and Desmos to plot and measure five structural proportions of the Parthenon, finding no evidence of the Golden Ratio (φ ≈ 1.618) in its construction. Extended the analysis to structural mechanics — applying Euler's Buckling Formula and compressive strength calculations (with a custom Python script for moment of inertia) to show the columns were engineered far beyond what the roof load required, suggesting aesthetics drove the design more than structural necessity.
